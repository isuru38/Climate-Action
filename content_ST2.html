What Is Sustainable Energy?

Sustainable energy comes from sources that are naturally replenished such as sunlight, wind, rain, tides, waves, and geothermal heat. Unlike fossil fuels, they do not emit greenhouse gases and are key to fighting climate change.

Environmental and Economic Benefits

Sustainable energy helps reduce pollution, slow climate change, and improve air and water quality. It also creates millions of green jobs and reduces dependence on imported fuels.

Leading Renewable Technologies

The main types of sustainable energy include solar, wind, hydroelectric, biomass, and geothermal. Each has unique advantages and can be adapted to local environments.

  • Solar Power: Converts sunlight into electricity.
  • Wind Energy: Uses wind turbines to generate power.
  • Hydroelectric: Harnesses water flow for electricity.
  • Geothermal: Taps heat from the earth’s core.

How You Can Help

You can support sustainable energy by reducing energy use, supporting green policies, installing solar panels, and choosing renewable energy plans where available.
